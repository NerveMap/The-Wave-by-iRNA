# Collaboration Guidelines

## Purpose
This document outlines how to propose and evaluate potential collaborations for The-Wave-by-iRNA project. Our goal is to foster meaningful, productive partnerships that advance the project while maintaining focus and realistic scope.

## What Makes a Good Collaboration Proposal

### Strong Proposals Include:
- **Clear Objectives**: Specific, measurable goals that align with project aims
- **Defined Scope**: Realistic boundaries on what will and won't be covered
- **Mutual Benefit**: Clear value proposition for all parties involved
- **Resource Assessment**: Honest evaluation of time, expertise, and resources needed
- **Timeline**: Reasonable schedule with milestones
- **Relevant Expertise**: Demonstrated knowledge or skills in the proposed area

### Red Flags (What to Avoid):
- Vague or overly ambitious goals without clear implementation plans
- Proposals lacking specific deliverables or outcomes
- Unrealistic timelines or resource requirements
- Lack of alignment with project focus areas
- Proposals based on assumptions rather than research or evidence
- Overly complicated schemes without clear benefit

## Collaboration Focus Areas

We are particularly interested in collaborations related to:

1. **RNA Analysis**
   - Sequence analysis methodologies
   - Pattern recognition in RNA data
   - Novel analysis algorithms

2. **Bioinformatics**
   - Data processing pipelines
   - Computational biology approaches
   - Statistical analysis methods

3. **Wave-Based Analysis**
   - Signal processing techniques
   - Wave pattern detection
   - Frequency domain analysis

4. **Data Visualization**
   - Interactive visualization tools
   - Real-time data representation
   - Scientific plotting libraries

5. **Research & Publications**
   - Joint research initiatives
   - Academic partnerships
   - Publication collaborations

## How to Submit a Collaboration Proposal

1. **Review Project Goals**: Ensure your proposal aligns with our focus areas
2. **Check Examples**: Review [good and bad proposal examples](COLLABORATION_EXAMPLES.md) 
3. **Use the Template**: Fill out the [Collaboration Proposal Template](COLLABORATION_PROPOSAL_TEMPLATE.md)
4. **Be Specific**: Provide concrete details, not general ideas
5. **Submit**: Create an issue on GitHub using the "Collaboration Proposal" label

## Evaluation Process

### Review Criteria:
- ✅ Alignment with project goals
- ✅ Feasibility and realistic scope
- ✅ Clear deliverables and outcomes
- ✅ Relevant expertise and resources
- ✅ Mutual benefit
- ✅ Well-defined timeline

### Timeline:
- Initial review: Within 7 days of submission
- Feedback provided: Within 14 days
- Follow-up discussion: As needed for promising proposals

## Feedback Mechanism

All proposals will receive constructive feedback, including:
- **Strengths**: What aspects are well-developed
- **Concerns**: Areas that need clarification or improvement
- **Suggestions**: How to strengthen the proposal
- **Decision**: Accept, request revisions, or decline with explanation

For detailed information about our feedback process, evaluation criteria, and timelines, see [FEEDBACK_PROCESS.md](FEEDBACK_PROCESS.md).

## Maintaining Focus

To ensure productive collaborations:
- We prioritize proposals with clear, achievable goals
- We value evidence-based approaches over speculative ideas
- We seek collaborators who can commit realistic time and resources
- We encourage iterative development over all-or-nothing proposals

## Questions?

If you have questions about collaboration opportunities:
1. Check if your question is answered in this document
2. Review existing collaboration proposals for examples
3. Open a discussion issue for general questions
4. Use the proposal template for specific collaboration ideas
