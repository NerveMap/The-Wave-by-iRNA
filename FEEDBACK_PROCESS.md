# Collaboration Feedback Process

## Overview
This document describes how collaboration proposals are reviewed and how feedback is provided to ensure a constructive, transparent process.

## Review Timeline

| Stage | Timeline | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| **Submission** | Day 0 | Proposal submitted via GitHub issue |
| **Acknowledgment** | Within 2 days | Confirmation that proposal was received |
| **Initial Review** | Within 7 days | First assessment of proposal |
| **Detailed Feedback** | Within 14 days | Comprehensive feedback provided |
| **Discussion** | As needed | Follow-up conversations for promising proposals |
| **Decision** | Within 21 days | Final decision communicated |

## Evaluation Criteria

Each proposal is evaluated against the following criteria:

### 1. Alignment (Weight: 25%)
- Does the proposal align with project focus areas?
- Is it relevant to RNA analysis, bioinformatics, or wave-based research?
- Does it complement existing project goals?

### 2. Feasibility (Weight: 25%)
- Is the scope realistic?
- Are the timelines achievable?
- Are required resources available or obtainable?

### 3. Clarity (Weight: 20%)
- Are objectives clearly defined?
- Are deliverables specific and measurable?
- Is the proposed approach well-explained?

### 4. Value (Weight: 15%)
- Does it provide mutual benefit?
- Will it advance the project meaningfully?
- Is there clear potential impact?

### 5. Expertise (Weight: 15%)
- Does the proposer have relevant skills/experience?
- Is there demonstrated capability to deliver?
- Are resources adequate for the proposed work?

## Feedback Categories

### ✅ Approved
**Criteria**: High scores across all evaluation criteria

**Next Steps**:
1. Detailed planning meeting scheduled
2. Collaboration agreement drafted
3. Milestones and checkpoints established
4. Work begins with regular check-ins

**Feedback Includes**:
- Specific strengths of the proposal
- Initial thoughts on implementation
- Suggested refinements for planning phase
- Point of contact for collaboration

### 🔄 Revisions Requested
**Criteria**: Good foundation but needs clarification or adjustments

**Next Steps**:
1. Specific revision requests provided
2. 7-day window for resubmission
3. Expedited re-review process
4. Decision on revised proposal within 7 days

**Feedback Includes**:
- What works well in the proposal
- Specific areas needing improvement
- Suggestions for strengthening the proposal
- Questions requiring clarification
- Examples of what stronger proposals might include

### ⏸️ Deferred
**Criteria**: Interesting but not aligned with current priorities or capacity

**Next Steps**:
1. Explanation of timing or capacity constraints
2. Suggestion to resubmit when conditions change
3. Invitation to stay connected
4. Timeline for potential reconsideration

**Feedback Includes**:
- Why the proposal is being deferred
- What would need to change for acceptance
- Alternative approaches or timing to consider
- Related opportunities that might be available

### ❌ Declined
**Criteria**: Not aligned with project goals or not feasible

**Next Steps**:
1. Clear explanation of decision
2. Constructive feedback on why it wasn't a fit
3. Suggestions for alternative directions if applicable
4. Appreciation for the interest

**Feedback Includes**:
- Specific reasons for declining
- Aspects of the proposal that were concerning
- What would make a stronger fit
- Encouragement to consider other opportunities

## Ensuring Constructive Feedback

Our feedback aims to be:

### Specific
❌ "This proposal is too vague"
✅ "The deliverables section needs specific outcomes. For example, instead of 'improve analysis', specify 'reduce processing time by 20%' or 'increase accuracy to 95%'"

### Actionable
❌ "The timeline doesn't work"
✅ "Consider extending Phase 2 from 2 to 4 weeks to allow for proper testing and iteration"

### Balanced
- We highlight strengths, not just weaknesses
- We explain both what works and what doesn't
- We provide context for our decisions

### Respectful
- We appreciate the effort put into proposals
- We assume good intent
- We focus on the proposal, not the person

## Red Flags We Watch For

To maintain focus and avoid "paranoid and delusional" proposals, we decline proposals that:

1. **Lack Specificity**
   - Vague goals like "revolutionize RNA research"
   - No concrete deliverables or outcomes
   - Undefined scope or methodology

2. **Are Unrealistic**
   - Overpromising on timeline or outcomes
   - Insufficient resources for proposed work
   - Lack of relevant expertise or experience

3. **Lack Focus**
   - Too broad in scope
   - Trying to solve too many problems at once
   - Not aligned with project focus areas

4. **Are Based on Assumptions**
   - Speculative ideas without research backing
   - Solutions looking for problems
   - Ignoring existing literature or approaches

5. **Have Unclear Value**
   - No mutual benefit identified
   - Unclear how it advances the project
   - Missing success metrics

## Appeal Process

If you disagree with the feedback or decision:

1. **Wait 48 Hours**: Take time to review the feedback objectively
2. **Address Concerns**: Prepare responses to specific concerns raised
3. **Submit Appeal**: Reply to the feedback with your clarifications
4. **Second Review**: A different reviewer will assess the appeal
5. **Final Decision**: Provided within 7 days of appeal

Appeals should include:
- Acknowledgment of the original feedback
- Specific responses to concerns raised
- New information or clarification
- Revised proposal if applicable

## Questions About Feedback?

If you need clarification on feedback received:
- Ask specific questions about the feedback
- Request examples or elaboration
- Seek guidance on how to improve

We're committed to providing helpful, constructive feedback that supports meaningful collaborations while maintaining project focus and quality standards.
